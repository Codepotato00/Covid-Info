ğŸ” The Basics of Viruses

Viruses are quite diverse, they display a huge variety of genomic structures.

ğŸ” They share a few features:

First, viruses are the smallest of all the microbes, with a diameter of less than 200 nanometers (nm). The COVID-19 virus is 0.125 microns.

Second, they can replicate only within a host cell. Viruses reproduce by infecting their host cells and reprogramming them to become virus-making factories.

Third, no known virus contains ribosomes, a necessary component of a cell's protein-making translational machinery.

Unlike all other biological entities, some viruses, like poliovirus, have RNA genomes.

Some of the herpesviruses have DNA genomes. Further, some viruses (like influenza virus) have single-stranded genomes, while others (like smallpox) have double-stranded genomes.
